What is dementia?


Contrary to popular belief, dementia is not actually a condition itself. It is an umbrella term used to describe a group of symptoms, which people would recognize as dementia.

Some of these symptoms include loss of memory, the decline in ability to think and reason and issues with communication.

Symptoms of dementia are caused by a variety of different disorders and conditions and can vary enormously between individuals.

Dementia is thought to currently affect up to 850,000 people in the UK, with this figure set to rise to 1,000,000 by 2025.
