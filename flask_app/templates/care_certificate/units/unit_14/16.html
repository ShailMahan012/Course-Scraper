Summary/reflection


You have now completed the first section. You have learnt:

  • Confidentiality is a crucial right of all individuals receiving care and support. It is also a legal requirement. It is essential to protect the individual and helps build relationships that are based on trust.
  • Personal and sensitive information must be treated confidentially and should only be shared on a need-to-know basis.
  • It is crucial to safeguard individuals’ personal information from being accidentally viewed or heard.
  • If you use any type of social media, you must be mindful of all individuals’ confidentiality rights, including others you work with. Always use these platforms responsibly.
  • As a health and social care worker, you have an overall responsibility to safeguard an individual’s personal information.
  • There are eight Caldicott Principles. These relate to the collection, use and sharing of confidential information in health and social care.
