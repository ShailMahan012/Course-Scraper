Case study – Victoria Climbie


Eight-year-old Victoria Climbie was tortured and murdered by her guardians after it transpired that her mother had been convinced that Victoria was possessed by the devil by a preacher at a local church.

Despite being seen by medical professionals and local authority services on several occasions before her death, links were not made between agencies and so the abuse of Victoria was allowed to continue.

Referrals were made about her safety but the family was thought to have ‘moved on’ when contact could not be made with them.

After two hospital admissions, Victoria died after being taken to hospital suffering from hypothermia, multiple organ failure and malnutrition.

Her death provoked a statutory inquiry and it was decided that a public inquiry should take place. It was determined that Victoria had been failed by multiple agencies and that the council under whose care she was placed was understaffed and poorly managed.

Her death also prompted changed in child protection laws in England, including the publishing of ‘Every Child Matters’ which brought about a change in legislation, leading to the Children Act 2004.
