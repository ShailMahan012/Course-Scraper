Summary/Reflection


You have now completed this section. You have learnt:

  • Airways can be obstructed by many different causes, e.g. foreign bodies, allergic reactions, asthma, blood, vomit and infections.
  • An obstruction in the airway can result in minor or major breathing difficulties, and unconsciousness and unresponsiveness, in severe circumstances.
  • Choking may result from either a partial or complete obstruction of the airway. The severity of the blockage will determine the difficulty in breathing.
  • No health and social care worker should administer back blows and abdominal thrusts unless they have successfully completed formal practical training.
  • Treatment for choking is different depending on whether the casualty is an adult, child or infant. The current RCUK guidance details the full procedures for choking treatment.
