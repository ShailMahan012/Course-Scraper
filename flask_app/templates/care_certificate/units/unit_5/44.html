Summary of Care Certificate Standard 5

You have now completed the Care Certificate Standard 5, you have covered a number of points including:

The 6Cs

You have learnt the six values that apply to health and social care workers and there definitions. They are known as the 6Cs: Care, Compassion, Competence, Communication, Courage and Commitment.

Working in a person-centred way

You now know that when you work with an individual, to plan their care and support, you are working in a person-centred way to meet their unique needs, Working in this way means that it reduces risk of negative, unfair or harmful treatment and neglect.

Reviewing and updating care plans

You have considered that care and support plans are a vital source of information. They must be regularly reviewed and updated to take into account individuals' changing needs and preferences. Therefore, care plans are dynamic records.

Planning for the future

You have learnt that it is vital that individuals are supported to plan for the future, and this can help to ensure their wellbeing and fulfilment. It can improve their quality of life, even when their care is short-term.

Advocacy

You now know that an advocate can communicate the individuals wishes on their behalf if they are unable to do so themselves.
