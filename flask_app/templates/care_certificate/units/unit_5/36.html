Summary/reflection


You have now completed this section. You have learnt:

  • There are things in the area around an individual, in their environment or actions you may carry out, that may cause them pain, discomfort or distress.
  • If the environment is causing the individual distress and it is not possible to action it immediately, you should speak to their carer or a manager for advice.
  • Consent is a crucial part of health and social care work, particularly where you have to carry out actions that may be unpleasant for the individual.
  • Handovers and team meetings are useful in highlighting any concerns regarding an individual’s distress and discomfort.
  • You should be able to recognise if an individual needs support. In addition to a person informing you they are in pain or discomfort, there are also non-verbal signs.
