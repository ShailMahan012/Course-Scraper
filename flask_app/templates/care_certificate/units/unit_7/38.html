Summary of Care Certificate Standard 7

You have now completed the Care Certificate Standard 7, you have covered a number of points including:

Mental capacity

You now know that occasionally, an individual may be incapable of understanding and retaining the information required to make decisions or communicate their choices. In these cases, they may be assessed as lacking the mental capacity to make decisions.

Person-centred values

You have considered that everything you do must be in the interests of the individual receiving care or support. This means working in a person-centred way and always protecting an individual's privacy and dignity.

Confidentiality

You have learned that by upholding confidentiality, it will help you build trust with the individual. However, there may be circumstances where you have to pass on the information for health and social care reasons.

Privacy dilemmas

You now know there may be instances where the individual does not want to share information which you believe should be passed to other workers to provide the care and support they need.

Privacy and dignity

You have learned that issues regarding privacy and dignity will vary in each workplace. You should discuss the specific issues with your manager or other workers to ensure that you understand what they are.
