Case study


Sarah is a 29-year-old home help, employed by an agency, caring for the elderly in the community, Sarah is 22 weeks pregnant and has just informed her manager.

At the end of her shift, the agency calls her and tells her the care company do not want her to work there anymore. The agency say they’ll try to find her other work, but she hears nothing from them.

Sarah could take action against both the care company and the agency, if the reason they’ve stopped giving her work is because she is pregnant.

Contract and agency workers are protected from discrimination under section 41 of the Equality Act 2010.
