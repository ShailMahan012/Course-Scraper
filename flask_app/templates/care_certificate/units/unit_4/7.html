How can I tell whether I personally discriminate against someone?


Being aware of their belief system and values can make someone more self-aware and therefore less likely to act in a discriminatory manner.

In order for someone to try and identify what their beliefs and values are, people may ask themselves some of the following questions:

1. Where do my beliefs and values come from?
2. What do I believe is most important in my life and why?
3. Do I consider the feelings and actions of others before reacting?
4. Do I judge people?
5. With what race do I identify?
6. What are my beliefs about marriages and relationships between people who are different races and religions?
7. What is my view on same-sex marriage?
8. Do I have a religion and do I practice? Why or why not?
9. Does my community have a shared religion?
10. What culture do I best identify with?
11. How much do I know about other cultures?
12. What is my political affiliation and why?
13. What are my views on equal rights?
14. Who do I believe is the same as me?
15. Who may I have passed my values and beliefs on to?
