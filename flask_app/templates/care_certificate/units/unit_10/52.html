Summary of Care Certificate Standard 10

You have now completed the Care Certificate Standard 10, you have covered a number of points including:

What is a vulnerable adult?

You now know that a vulnerable adult is classed as an individual who is aged 18 years or over and who, for any reason, is unable to take care of themselves or to protect themselves against any form of harm, abuse or exploitation.

Duty of care

You have learned that a duty of care is a moral or legal obligation to ensure the safety and well-being of others. This means that any organisation which provides care and support to vulnerable adults, must put the interests of the people who use those services first.

The 6 principles of adult safeguarding

You have considered the 6 principles of adult safeguarding that is set out by The Care Act 2014: empowerment, prevention, proportionality, protection, partnership and accountability.

Reporting, recording and referring information and preserving evidence

You now know that it is the responsibility of the employee to make sure that they are aware of the content of a policy, which relates to all four of the priorities: protect, report and refer, preserve and record.

Checking existing reports

You have learned that if there is an existing report then its content may be used to corroborate allegations at a later time, such as if a criminal charge is brought against an abuser who has been previously convicted.
