Case study Kim and Louise


Kim has had a diagnosis of schizophrenia since she was 18 years old. Aged now 25, she still has significant difficulties in controlling its symptoms, one of which is being unable to distinguish fantasy from reality.

She resigned from her job after taking a lot of time off and being unable to manage the stress that it brought, which made her symptoms worse.

On the recommendation of her GP, Kim attends a support group where people with mental health difficulties come together to speak about their illness and to get support from each other.

The group is supervised by Louise who is a qualified social worker who specialises in mental illness.

Louise is aware of Kim’s symptoms and often hears her speak of things with other individuals, which are obviously fantasy and accepts that these are just part of Kim’s illness which she is trying to get under control.
