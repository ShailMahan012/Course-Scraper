Summary of Care Certificate Standard 11

You have now completed the Care Certificate Standard 11, you have covered a number of points including:

What is Safeguarding?

You now know that The Care Act 2014 defines safeguarding as “protecting an adult’s (or child’s) right to live in safety, free from abuse and neglect.”

Legislation and policy

You have learned that legislation and policy exist to ensure that children have rights and that these rights are upheld so that they can live their life free from abuse, harm and exploitation.

The definition of abuse

You have considered that the actual definition of abuse maintains that it is “persistently treating someone with cruelty or violence in one or more ways especially regularly or repeatedly.”

Types of disclosures

You now know that children may disclose abuse in one or more of several different methods for example: direct disclosure, indirect disclosure, behavioural disclosure and non-verbal disclosure.

Suspicions of abuse

You have learned that even without any form of disclosure, any person can report a suspicion of abuse, a concern may be told to: a midwife, GP, teacher etc.
