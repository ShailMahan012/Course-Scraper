Summary/reflection


You have now completed this section. You have learnt:

  • Values are beliefs and ideas about how people should behave and are formed by our childhoods, families, backgrounds, cultures, religions and relationships.
  • The 6Cs apply to health and social care workers. These are care, compassion, competence, communication, courage and commitment.
  • Person-centred values tell you how to work in a person-centred way. These values are individuality, independence, privacy, partnership, choice, dignity, respect and rights.
  • When you work in a person-centred way, you put the individual at the centre of their care and support so they can choose and control how they want it to be.
  • The delivery of person-centred care focuses on priorities, such as compassion, dignity and respect, shared decision-making and public involvement.
