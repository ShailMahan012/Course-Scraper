Summary/reflection


You have now completed the final section. You have learnt:

  • In health and social care, confidentiality is a vital principle that forms the basis of codes of practice and all ways of working.
  • For an individual to give their informed consent, they must have all of the required information to make a considered decision.
  • An individual has rights regarding the confidentiality of their personal and sensitive information.
  • When it comes to an individual’s confidentiality, you must abide by the principles of the Data Protection Act 2018, UK GDPR and the Human Rights Act 1998.
  • There may be instances where information that is normally considered to be confidential needs to be shared and confidentiality has to be broken.
  • Regardless of how you are communicating with an individual, you should always be respectful, try to match methods to the needs of the individual and be aware of confidentiality.
